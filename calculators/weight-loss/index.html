How Long Will It Take to Reach Your Goal?

Planning a weight loss journey requires realistic timelines. Losing weight too quickly can damage your metabolic rate, lead to muscle loss, and increase the likelihood of weight regain. A safe, clinical rate of loss is 1 to 2 pounds per week.

This calculator factors in your starting weight, target weight, and daily calorie deficit to map out a precise weekly timeline. Creating a moderate deficit through diet and exercise allows you to shed body fat steadily and sustainably.

Remember that weight loss is rarely linear. Fluctuations in water retention, muscle soreness, hormonal cycles, and sodium intake can cause your scale weight to stall or jump temporarily. Focus on long-term trends rather than daily scale changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a healthy weekly weight loss?

A rate of 0.5 to 2.0 pounds (0.25 to 1.0 kg) per week is considered safe, sustainable, and optimal for body composition.

Q: Why did my weight loss stall?

Stalls are normal and usually caused by temporary water retention or a drop in TDEE as you lose weight. Adjust your calorie target if the stall lasts more than 3-4 weeks.
