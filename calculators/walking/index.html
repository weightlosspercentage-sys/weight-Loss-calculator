How Walking Calories Are Calculated

Exercise scientists measure physical activity intensity in **Metabolic Equivalents (METs)**. One MET represents the rate at which your body expends energy while resting. Walking at a moderate speed of 3.0 mph has a MET value of 3.5. This means you burn 3.5 times more energy than sitting still.

The clinical formula to determine calorie burn is:

Calories = (MET × 3.5 × Body Weight in kg) ÷ 200 × Duration in Minutes

Step and Deficit Strategy

Integrating walking with a dietary deficit is the most sustainable approach to fat loss. For instance, walking 10,000 steps covers roughly 4-5 miles, burning between 300 to 500 calories depending on your body weight. Combining this active expenditure with a moderate deficit established using our calorie deficit calculator can double your fat loss speed.
