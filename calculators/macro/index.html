How to Track Macronutrients for Body Composition

Macronutrients, or macros, are the nutritional building blocks of your diet: proteins, carbohydrates, and fats. While total calorie intake determines whether you lose or gain weight, your macro distribution dictates whether that weight change comes from fat or muscle.

Each macronutrient has a specific role and caloric value: protein provides 4 calories per gram and preserves muscle mass; carbohydrates provide 4 calories per gram and fuel workouts; fats provide 9 calories per gram and support hormone health.

Customizing your macronutrient ratios allows you to target specific fitness goals. For example, a ketogenic diet requires high fat and minimal carbs, while muscle growth requires elevated protein and moderate carbohydrates. Our calculator makes it easy to set and track these targets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best macro ratio for weight loss?

A common macro split for fat loss is 30% to 40% protein, 30% to 40% carbohydrates, and 20% to 30% fats. Hitting your protein target is the most important factor.

Q: How many calories are in macros?

Protein and carbohydrates contain 4 calories per gram, while fats contain 9 calories per gram.
