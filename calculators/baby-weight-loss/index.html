Baby Growth Tracking and Percentages

Calculating your baby's weight changes as a percentage helps standardize tracking, especially when monitoring feeding progress. This percentage measures changes relative to birth weight or peak weight.

Healthy growth is supported by responsive feeding, whether breastfeeding or using formula. Keeping a log of weight changes, wet diapers, and sleep patterns helps parents understand their baby's health trends.

This tool is designed to provide clear calculations and educational guidelines. Any concerns regarding your baby's weight, feeding, or development should be discussed directly with a medical professional.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do you calculate baby weight loss percentage?

Subtract the baby's current weight from their birth weight, divide by the birth weight, and multiply by 100.

Q: Is baby weight loss normal?

Only in the first 3-4 days of life. Subsequent weight loss or a failure to gain weight requires clinical evaluation.
