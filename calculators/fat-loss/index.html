Body Composition Science

Your total body weight is divided into two primary categories: **Fat Mass** (adipose tissue) and **Lean Body Mass** (muscle, bone, water, and organs). Weight loss percentage calculators only measure total scale weight loss, which can mask muscle wasting. Tracking body composition ensures that you are losing fat rather than structural tissue.

How the Calculation Works

Assuming your lean body mass remains constant (the ideal scenario, which requires strength training and sufficient protein), we calculate the targets as follows:

  1. Lean Body Mass (LBM) = Current Weight × (1 − (Current Body Fat % ÷ 100))
  2. Target Body Weight = LBM ÷ (1 − (Target Body Fat % ÷ 100))
  3. Fat to Lose = Current Weight − Target Body Weight

Preventing Muscle Loss

To preserve your metabolic rate and ensure your weight loss is pure fat, you must:

  • Maintain a moderate calorie deficit. Avoid drop diets that cause rapid muscle wasting. Calculate your caloric goals using our calorie deficit calculator.
  • Consume sufficient protein. A daily protein intake target is essential when in a deficit. Use our protein calculator to find your target.
  • Incorporate resistance training to preserve lean tissues.
