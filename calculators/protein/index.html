Calculating Daily Protein Requirements

Protein is the primary macronutrient responsible for repairing tissues, building skeletal muscle, and producing hormones. Consuming adequate protein is essential during a weight loss phase because it protects your active muscle tissue from being broken down for energy.

Dietary protein is also highly satiating. It has a high thermic effect of food (TEF), meaning your body burns roughly 20-30% of the calories consumed from protein just to digest and process it, compared to only 5-15% for carbs and fats.

Sedentary adults require a minimum of 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight. However, active individuals, lifters, and those in a calorie deficit should consume 1.6 to 2.2 grams of protein per kilogram (0.7 to 1.0 gram per pound) to optimize muscle preservation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much protein do I need to build muscle?

Aim for 0.7 to 1.0 gram of protein per pound of body weight (1.6 to 2.2 grams per kg) daily, combined with progressive resistance training.

Q: Can you eat too much protein?

For healthy individuals, high protein intake is safe. Excess protein is processed by the kidneys and used for energy or stored.
