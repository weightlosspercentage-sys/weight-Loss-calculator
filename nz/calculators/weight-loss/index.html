How to Use This Weight Loss Calculator

Getting your personalized weight loss plan takes under 60 seconds:

  1. Enter your current weight — toggle between kg and lbs.
  2. Enter your goal weight — a safe starting target is 5–10% of your current body weight.
  3. Add your height and age — these determine your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR).
  4. Select your activity level — when in doubt, go one level lower than you think.
  5. Set a target date or weekly rate — the calculator works both ways.

Your results instantly show your daily calorie target, weekly projected loss, estimated weeks to goal, and weight loss percentage.

The Science Behind Your Results

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — the most accurate formula for estimating resting energy expenditure, validated in peer-reviewed research and recommended by nutrition authorities worldwide.

How Your Calorie Target Is Calculated

  1. BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): How many calories your body burns at complete rest.
  2. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): Your BMR multiplied by an activity factor — the actual calories you burn each day.
  3. Calorie Deficit: Eat less than your TDEE. A deficit of ~550 cal/day ≈ 0.5 kg of fat lost per week.

NZ Ministry of Health Recommended Rate of Weight Loss

The NZ Ministry of Health recommends losing 0.5 to 1 kg per week. This preserves muscle mass, avoids nutrient deficiencies, and produces lasting results.

What Is Weight Loss Percentage — And Why Does It Matter?

Raw kilograms lost tells only part of the story. Weight loss percentage shows how much of your starting body weight you've lost — the metric doctors and clinical researchers actually use.

The Formula

Weight Loss % = ((Starting Weight − Current Weight) ÷ Starting Weight) × 100

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my weight loss percentage?

Subtract your current weight from your starting weight, divide by your starting weight, then multiply by 100. Example: starting at 90 kg, now 81 kg → ((90−81)÷90)×100 = 10%. Or use our step-by-step guide.

What is a healthy rate of weight loss?

The NZ Ministry of Health recommends 0.5 to 1 kg per week. Faster loss increases the risk of muscle loss, nutrient deficiencies, and weight regain.

How long does it take to lose 10 kg?

At 0.5 kg/week: approximately 20 weeks (5 months). At 1 kg/week: about 10 weeks. Enter your stats in the calculator above to get your exact personalized timeline.

Why did my weight loss stall?

As you lose weight, your TDEE decreases — your body burns fewer calories doing the same activities. Reassess your calorie target every 4–6 weeks using this calculator with your updated current weight.

What is the difference between BMR and TDEE?

BMR is the calories your body burns at complete rest. TDEE is your BMR multiplied by your activity level — the actual calories you burn each day. Your weight loss target is always calculated from TDEE, not BMR.
