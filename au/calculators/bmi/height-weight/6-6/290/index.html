Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does a BMI of 33.5 mean for someone 6'6" tall?

A BMI of 33.5 for a 6'6" individual classifies you as Obese Class I. Class I obesity substantially increases risk of cardiovascular disease, Type 2 diabetes, sleep apnea, and joint disorders. Clinical weight management is recommended.

Q: What is the ideal weight for someone 6'6" tall?

For a height of 6'6", the healthy weight range is 160 to 215 lbs (BMI 18.5 to 24.9). Use our BMI calculator to check any weight.

Q: How is BMI calculated for 6'6" 290 lbs?

BMI = (weight in lbs ÷ height in inches²) × 703. For 6'6" (78 inches) and 290 lbs: (290 ÷ 78²) × 703 = 33.5.

Q: How can I lower my BMI from 33.5 to a healthy range?

To reach a healthy BMI, you would need to lose approximately 75 lbs. A safe rate is 0.5 to 2 lbs per week through a moderate calorie deficit. Use our weight loss calculator to create a personalized plan.
